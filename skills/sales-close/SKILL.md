# Sales Close SOP

## 1. Dream 100 Strategy

Identify and pursue your highest-value prospects (source: Chet Holmes, "The Ultimate Sales Machine"):

- **Build the list**: Identify 100 ideal customers who would transform your business. Quality over quantity — these are companies/people who already have the exact problem you solve.
- **Deep research each**: Role, company stage, recent news, tech stack, public pain points. Spend 10-15 min per prospect before any outreach.
- **Multi-channel persistence**: Email + LinkedIn + events + referrals. Chet Holmes found that 95% of a market was controlled by just 167 companies — targeting them specifically doubled sales within 12-15 months.
- **Sell the meeting, not the product**: First outreach goal is a conversation, not a close. Offer an educational session or insight, not a pitch.
- **Cadence**: Touch each prospect every 2-4 weeks across channels. Most deals close after 5-12 touches. Most salespeople give up after 2.

## 2. Cold Email Formula

Structure for 2025-2026 outbound (source: Saleshandy, Autobound, Cleverly, Mailshake):

**Single email structure:**
- **Subject**: Under 5 words, no clickbait ("Quick question", "Saw your [specific thing]")
- **Opening**: Reference something specific about them — hiring post, funding round, product launch, role change. Never generic flattery.
- **Value prop**: One sentence. What you do + specific result ("We help [role] at [company type] reduce [problem] by [metric]")
- **CTA**: Low-friction ask. Not "book a demo" — instead "worth a 15-min chat?" or "want me to send a 2-min video walkthrough?"
- **PS line**: Optional social proof ("PS: We helped [similar company] achieve [specific result]")

**3-email sequence:**
| Email | Timing | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Day 0 | Value prop + low-friction CTA |
| 2 | Day 3-5 | New angle or case study, reply to thread |
| 3 | Day 8-10 | Breakup email ("Should I close your file?") |

**Deliverability (non-negotiable in 2026):**
- Configure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC before sending anything — major providers reject emails without these
- Warm up new domains for 2-4 weeks before outbound
- Keep volume under 50 emails/day per mailbox
- Target 95%+ deliverability rate
- 50-125 words per email gets highest reply rates

**Benchmarks**: Average reply rate is 3.4%. Signal-based personalized campaigns achieve 15-25% (source: Autobound, Instantly 2026 data).

## 3. SPIN Questions

Use in demos and sales calls (source: Neil Rackham, "SPIN Selling" — based on 35,000+ sales call study):

- **Situation**: Gather facts about their current state. "How do you currently handle [process]?" "What tools are you using for [task]?" Keep these brief — too many situation questions bore prospects.
- **Problem**: Uncover pain points. "What's the biggest challenge with [current approach]?" "Where does the process break down?" These reveal implied needs.
- **Implication**: Expand the pain. "What happens when [problem] occurs?" "How does that affect [team/revenue/timeline]?" "What's the cost of not solving this?" These are the most powerful questions — they make the problem feel urgent.
- **Need-payoff**: Let them articulate the value. "If you could [solve problem], what would that mean for your team?" "How would [outcome] impact [metric]?" The prospect sells themselves.

Key insight from Rackham's research: Traditional closing techniques hurt large sales. The best sellers ask better questions, they don't push harder.

## 4. Objection Handling

Framework: Listen → Acknowledge → Reframe → Resolve (source: Cognism, HubSpot, Salesforce):

| Objection | Response strategy |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Price** | "What's the cost of NOT solving this? If [problem] costs you [X/month], this pays for itself in [timeframe]." |
| **Timing** | "What changes in 3 months that makes it a better time? Usually the cost of waiting is [specific consequence]." |
| **Competition** | "Great that you're evaluating options. Here's specifically how we differ on [their stated priority]." Don't trash competitors — differentiate. |
| **Need** | "Let me show you what users in your situation discovered. [Share specific case study]." |
| **Authority** | "Totally understand. What would [decision maker] need to see? I can prepare a summary for them." |
| **Trust** | "Fair concern. Here's [case study / free trial / pilot program] so you can verify before committing." |

The "Feel, Felt, Found" backup: "I understand how you feel. [Company X] felt the same way. What they found was [specific outcome]."

## 5. Pipeline Management

Keep it simple, especially as a solo founder (source: general B2B sales practice):

**Stages:**
1. **Lead** — matches ICP, not yet contacted
2. **Contacted** — outreach sent, awaiting response
3. **Conversation** — replied, actively discussing
4. **Demo/Trial** — experiencing the product
5. **Proposal** — pricing/terms sent
6. **Closed Won / Closed Lost** — track both with reason codes

**Tracking**: Spreadsheet is fine until ~50 active deals. Then use a free CRM (HubSpot Free, Attio, Folk). The tool matters less than the discipline of updating it.

**Weekly review**: Every Friday, review pipeline. Move stale deals (no response in 14+ days) to "Closed Lost — No Response." Focus energy on deals in Demo/Proposal stage.

## 6. Solo Founder Sales Rules

Constraints that protect your time and sanity:

- **Only sell to people who already have the problem** — if you have to convince them the problem exists, walk away
- **Never hard-sell** — desperate energy repels buyers. Be genuinely helpful and let them decide.
- **Time-box sales to 1 hour/day max** — protect your building time. Sales won't matter if the product doesn't improve.
- **Qualify ruthlessly** — ask "do you have budget and authority to buy this quarter?" early. Don't waste weeks on tire-kickers.
- **Automate the top of funnel** — content, SEO, and product-led growth feed the pipeline while you sleep. Reserve outbound for Dream 100 targets only.
- **Track your numbers** — emails sent, replies, demos, closes. You can't improve what you don't measure.
- **Ask for referrals** — after every successful close, ask "who else do you know facing this problem?" Warm intros convert 3-5x better than cold outreach.
